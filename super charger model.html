Modeling of an Eaton M62 Roots-Type Supercharger

Project Summary:

Objective: To create a detailed, fully-constrained 3D CAD assembly of an Eaton M62 Roots-type supercharger by modeling each component from technical references and engineering principles.

Core Challenge: Accurately model complex geometry (especially the helical rotor lobes) and assemble all components with proper mechanical relationships and clearances.

Outcome: A complete, functional CAD assembly demonstrating understanding of supercharger architecture, component interaction, and mechanical assembly principles in SOLIDWORKS.


Exploded view of the Eaton M62 supercharger assembly, showing all major components and their relative positioning.

Component Breakdown: Parts and Functions:

1. Casing (Main Body):
The casing is the central housing machined from aluminum alloy. It features: • Base Rotor Cavities: Two precisely machined bores that house the rotor lobes.
• Intake Port: Large opening where atmospheric or compressed air enters.
• Discharge Port: Outlet where compressed air exits to the intake manifold.
• Mounting Flanges: For attaching the supercharger to the engine and intercooler piping.
• Bearing Journals: Supports for the rotor shafts.

Casing/Housing - The primary structural component that contains the rotors and defines the intake and discharge ports.

2. Casing Cover (Front Plate):
This aluminum cover plate: • Seals the front of the rotor chamber
• Supports the front bearings for both rotor shafts.
• Mounts the pulley assembly.
• Contains the front oil seal for the drive shaft.

The front enclosure that seals the rotor assembly and supports the drive pulley.

3. Rotor Lobes (CW & CCW Helical Rotors):
These are the most complex components: • Three-Lobe Design: Each rotor has three helical lobes that mesh without touching.
• Helical Twist: Typically 60° helical angle for smooth air delivery.
• Timing Gears: Integral or attached to shaft ends for precise phasing.
• Clearance: Maintains 0.1-0.3mm clearance between lobes and casing.

Helical Rotor Pair (Clockwise and Counter-Clockwise) - The heart of the supercharger that moves and compresses air.

4. Timing Gears:
• Prevents Contact: Ensures rotors never touch each other.
• Maintains Timing: Keeps rotors in precise 90° phase relationship.
• Lubricated: Runs in oil bath for durability.
• Backlash Controlled: Typically 0.05-0.1mm for quiet operation.

Timing Gears - Precision gears that maintain proper phasing between rotors.

5. Gear Housing / Intermediate Plate:
This critical internal component serves multiple functions: • Chamber Separation: Creates a barrier between the dry rotor chamber (where air flows) and the wet gear chamber (oil-bathed gears).
•&nbsGear Mounting: Contains precise bearing bores for both rotor shafts where the timing gears are mounted.
• Shaft Seal Surface: Features machined surfaces for lip seals or o-rings that prevent oil migration into the air stream.
• Oil Management: Often includes oil return passages and splash protection features.
• Structural Support: Adds rigidity to the overall assembly between the main casing and front cover.

Gear Housing (Intermediate Plate) - Separates the rotor chamber from the gear chamber and provides mounting for the timing gears.

6. Pulley Assembly:
• Belt Interface: 6-rib serpentine belt surface.
• Hub: Connects to rotor shaft via key or spline.
• Size Variation: Different diameters for boost pressure adjustment.

Drive Pulley - Transfers engine power via serpentine belt to the supercharger.

7. Hex Bolts (Fasteners):
•  M8 x 1.25 or similar metric threads.
•  Grade 10.9 or higher for high clamp load.
•  Quantity: Typically 8-12 bolts around perimeter.

Hex Head Cap Screws - Secure the casing cover and retainer to the main housing.

Component Breakdown: Parts and Functions:

•  The helical lobe design reduces air pulsation compared to straight lobes.
•  Clearance management is critical—too little causes contact, too much reduces efficiency.
•  The timing gears are more precise than the lobes themselves.
•  Thermal expansion must be considered in clearance calculations.
•  The assembly demonstrates modular design principles for serviceability.
